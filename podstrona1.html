Protaetia cuprea
Geographic scope:
It inhabits all of Europe, including the areas of northern Fennoscandia, and part of Asia, reaching east to Siberia and the coast of the Pacific Ocean. Found throughout Poland, sometimes in large numbers, both in the lowlands and in the mountains, where it reaches the upper border of the forest.
Habitat and activity:
A species found in deciduous forests and their surroundings. Adult insects appear from June to August and are heliophilic - they fly in sunny weather. They willingly take food from flowers of shrubs and herbaceous plants as well as from ripe fruits. They also come to sap leaking from damaged trees.
